My Film Review of Free Guy

Posted on 2021-09-27

The newly released movie “Free Guy” exceeded my expectations. I thought it was just a well-conceived entertainment movie, but after watching it, it caused me to think. People play the game Free City where the players all wear ‘sunglasses’ and the NPCs can only see the scenes set within the game. Finally, one day, an NPC awakened to self-awareness: why am I set to live like this? NPCs in the game are bullied by players every day, such as being robbed of banks, killed, etc. These are all tasks of the players. This seems to be the mechanics of the game, but projected into reality, we will find the connection between the two.

In fact, in reality, we often live our lives the same way all over the days, repeat and repeat. Get up in the morning, go to work, do the same thing every day, go home from work, have fun, and then start a new day. Have we ever wondered why life is so set in stone? I had this complaint when I was a student.

I think the glasses in the movie represent the “information gap”. After NPC Guy grabbed a pair of glasses, he realized that the world in front of him was completely different from his own: gold coins floating on the street, medical kits… considering about there are people in your life see more things than you… they definitely have a better life.

From another perspective, the movie director may also do evaluation of the society. Are people with glasses can just hurt other people? Is it right for powerful guys do whatever they want to normal people?

The answer is no. But how many of them have enjoyed their privilege and hurting others? We have to find our own values, otherwise we just the NPCs for the world of ‘game’.

Law falls in Afghanistan

Posted on 2021-09-12

It’s hard for me to think that laws exist in a place like Afghanistan, or that there are places in the world that are so backward and centralized. It is also because the Taliban is in power that I am concerned about the country. The U.S. military has withdrawn from Afghanistan in disarray. When the troops retreated, many Afghans wanted to get on planes, even if they died, rather than stay in a country where the Taliban were in power. This reminds me of what I learned about Rwanda in my history books. 1994, the United Nation withdrew from the Taliban. They originally wanted to keep the peace in Rwanda, but when they got to the front line, they realized that they could not intervene in internal affairs except for self-defense. In my opinion a similar historical event is repeating itself.

The question is: what kind of power can fight against centralized power? I don’t think democracy can exist in such a place. Because people do not take the responsibility to protect the country, they naturally will not be responsible for this country. Democracy used to belong to a minority. Of course, in a modern society, especially a stable situation, where people have stable jobs and houses, such a society is suitable for overall democracy. But for backward countries, democracy would be a nightmare. From a realistic point of view, Afghanistan does not even have laws anymore. I mean, modern developed laws. The current system in Afghanistan can only be considered as traditional or oppression under strong power. The streets are filled with guns, the status of women is once again degraded, they have to wear black veil on the street and banned to receive education. They cannot work. Cannot communicate with even strangers.And it is legal to be domestically abused.

I believe that the current Afghanistan is a society where the law of the jungle is enforced.

I hope things will go well in the future.

Is Torture Ever Justified Essay?

Posted on 2021-08-30

This is related to my Extend Essay about terrorism, the 9.11 event. The terrorism” label has both broad and specific implications when it is applied. Altheide (2006) found that terrorism goes beyond a simple narrative, rather when an incident is labeled as terrorism, it becomes the definition of the situation. This framing of violence creates a sense that all Americans are potential victims of evil terrorists, and the military are portrayed as our protectors against terrorist violence.

The idea then arises to use torture on terrorists to make them punishable or to force them to reveal their plans for the rest of their terrorist acts. However, there is no current international law that supports such an act. We cannot be sure that some countries are privy to lynchings, but in general, the torture of terrorists is generally condemned.

There are many scholars who are calling for the legalization of the punishment of terrorists. They give theories and they give arguments. They cite an extreme example: If a plane is threatened to crash, should a knowing terrorist be tortured into telling the truth? If there are more than a hundred people on this one plane, are their lives not worthy of being cared for?

This example seems reasonable at first glance, but it is not without its flaws. First, the conditions are such that the probability of such a terrorist event is very small. Second, we cannot be sure that a terrorist with an informed plan could be captured under such conditions. Assuming these conditions are met, there is no guarantee that the terrorist is telling the truth. People tend to lie under pressure. If you can only use torture to extract a confession, then the likelihood of him lying is also very high. In principle, the probability of saving a planeload of people becomes much smaller.

In addition, modern society has provided a lot of technology. The use of torture is indeed one way, but it is barbaric and primitive. One doctor said that using torture on them would reduce us morally to the same kind of people as them, which is not fundamentally different. People are turning themselves into what they fear. There is an old Chinese saying, “A gentleman is far from the kitchen,” and one might ask, is it unjust for us to simply acquiesce to such actions?

I cannot give an answer. But legalizing torture obviously requires a lot of discussion. The law cannot yet be fully controlled by human beings, let alone directly disposing of the lives of others. In principle we do not have the right to do so, but reality is often not what it should be, and ideal ideologies cannot always be put into action.

Mock Trial tournament My first offline tutorial experience

Posted on 2021-08-10

This summer, I was the tutor for our mock trial team. This is the first official time that I led a new team to the city tournament, facing the opponents from all over the country, I got much pressure. I taught them all the rules and skills for a long time, we prepared for the moment since the winter holiday(online tournament), and it’s new for them to debate in the real court.

Before the night of the competition, I discussed with them about the acting skill for the witness. Actually, I was a bit concern for my juniors. Last year when I went to the MT tournament with my team, we researched for the whole night of those objections. No one instruct us about the rules of evidence, and we had to learn by ourselves. However, through our efforts and combined with the past experience, the objections lists were made by us, at least I am very familiar with the basic rules. While for our juniors, they used the files directly without thinking about why.

We had Tencent meeting online several times, I made screenshot for my notes but they seemed still have difficulties to memorize the rule of evidence. On the night I was very nervous, but pretended that I was fine. It was even more nervous than the time of last year. I looked upon their files and read through them, those direct examinations, cross, impeachment, which written beautifully on their little cards, a delight lightened inside of me.

Finally I told myself tomorrow will not be counted into the score, I should not help them fix their problems in the DX or CX. They ought to find them by themselves, at least experienced the feelings of being objections on court. I can handle the objections (especially for the experts) because last winter, I was aggressively objected by my opposing counsels. I remembered such feeling till now, failed to say nothing on court and waited until the time passed by, totally embarrassed not only me but also my teammates. After that, I was even keener to do expert witnesses. I will grow up where I fail.
