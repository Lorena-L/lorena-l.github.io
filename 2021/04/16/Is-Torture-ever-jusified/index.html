Is Torture ever jusified

Posted on 2021-04-16

In school, I selected English as my extended essay subject, and my research question was, to what extent does the American film industry affected public attitudes towards suspected terrorists. However, in the very beginning, my topic was is torture ever justified. I have read the international laws concerning torture, and it is banned by most of the country. But I started to think, torture, defined as pain, suffering, using an interrogation device, led to a continuum and the 2 extremes are on one hand torturing somebody to death; or non-lethal torture leaves on mental scars, causing unbearable pain but not long damage. I wondered if it’s real that the intention of international law to prohibit torture only out of the moral and ethical ground, or out of other reasons? Maybe it sounds like a conspiracy theory; however, the real purpose underneath the prohibition can be politically correct. Since in nowadays society, people merely have the right to be extreme or hate something. Thoughts and emotions can be rather private, but you just cannot speak out loud you support some actions or ideology which against the mainstream. Philosophers who supported banned torture hold the opinion that using torture techniques made no difference with terrorists, while the against ideas provide an occasion which states the efficiency of using torture when innocents are facing the threat from the terrorist. They give out an extreme example of a terrorist bombing an airplane, but from a statistics perspective, the probability for such an event to happen is tiny. However, those who supported torture offer us several benefits of torture in an emergency. Firstly, gathering the information quickly. Second, it’s fast and effective. From the first sight you may think those advantages are reasonable, but think it carefully, how can you examine the truthfulness of what the person says under extreme situations? He/she may lie and says what you want to hear. But as you can see all of the above statements are hypothesis, we have to use evidences to support these ideas.
