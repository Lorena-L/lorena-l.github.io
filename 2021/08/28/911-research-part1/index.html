Posted on 2021-08-28

What is terrorism? Before we get into the details of everything, I think we need to be clear about our topic. The study of terrorism is currently limited by the lack of consistency in the definition of what constitutes a terrorist act and a terrorist organisation, with some scholars arguing for some consistency in the criteria. Some scholars argue for consistency in criteria, others use the ideal typology of terrorism, and still others continue to believe that these debates are ultimately inconclusive. The most direct explanation is, Terrorism is commonly classified as “violence or the threat of violence against persons or property to intimidate a government or civilian population in pursuit of apolitical, social, economic, or religious goal” (refer from FBI 2005). and all of these arguments came after the a terrorist——the 9/11.

Throughout the whole event of 911 was high concerned by the whole society from 2001 till now and its left many people with horror impressions. The shadow of such event still exists. When the media focused on 911 a lot, writing about the big news, reporting and analyzing the facts, reassuring the families of the victims and survivors, the film industry didn’t just stop either. The films centered on 9/11 were no longer entertainment, but serious documents. The whole city is still deeply engulfed in horror, and even after a long time, people can’t forget it. The film helps to ease people’s emotions while allowing them to reflect in depth. Does the public have experience in facing terrorist organizations? Does the government have emergency measures in place? We should look fear in the face, face it and address it, rather than just avoid it.
