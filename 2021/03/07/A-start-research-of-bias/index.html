A start research of bias

Posted on 2021-03-07

Recently i have read some passages which related to the topic of whether the mainstream media is liberally biased nowadays. The points of view in the passage are rather interesting, where both sides have given out their own opinions and using evidences to prove their ideas. Since I am an Asian and will go to Europe or American to study in the future, I am interested in the different cultural groups which in the anglophone countries. I used to heard about some gossips about how Asians are badly treated in the circle, and that’s remind me of how black people faced discrimination in the last century, but the situation seems to be better now…is that true? Although we can see a lot of colleges lower their standards to black people, is it really shows the respect? There are many questions can be discovered from the issue, and the issue is globally. I won’t talk about this topic in detail in this passage but in the future i will.

The reason why i think about the question actually comes from a tiny affair of the art festival in our school. I am participating into it as an vocal who sing rap songs. However, my parents seem to dislike rap culture. I have been practiced the songs for about whole week. To be honest, I did not like rap culture before, but this song changed my mind. Rap is very hard to sing because there is a lot of rhythm in it and the whole speed is very fast. I have done some research before of the new culture, the data around me shows that the last generation more likely to refuse sub culture than the new generation. Also, most of the people seems to have a bias on the rap because the origin was from the black people in the slum and the lyrics were simple and dirty, hence they hold an unappreciated mind towards it. However, nowadays there are an amount of rap singers who are white people and you can see, yellow group. I would say that rap is a form of expressing the ideas and feelings rather than a certain sub culture, because everyone can just simply write their personal stories and speak it out loudly with rhyme.
