my first time being a main judge

Posted on 2021-12-12

In December I officiated at a school mock court competition. This is my first time being a main judge in a competition. To be honest, I was really nervous for my judgement. I expected there’s no conditions that beyond my current knowledge of objection. When I made the decision of overruled or sustain, not only should I telling the result but also to demonstrate the reason. I was largely influenced by a judge that I met in my grade ten. At that time i was not familiar with the procedure of entering exhibit, she taught me right in the court, for educational purpose. She treated every participant fairly and pointed out our fault so that we can learn faster. I will follow her steps.

After the tournament,I went to the judge interview of the tournament in Winter NUMT.(National United Mock Trial). I was prepared for the rule of evidence a lot, thinking about the interviewer may ask me specific question on the objection. Out of my expectation, he asked me what construct good opening statement and a closing argument. At the end of the interview, I was queried about how I saw the role of a judge and the difference between a real judge and a virtual judge. I believe that judges in competitions focus on deciding cases, whereas real judges are supposed to keep the case fair and ensure that justice is executed properly. I mentioned the rationale for the existence of juries. The person who interviewed me told me that he would have preferred to hear that the judges were also biased. This was something I had not expected and I found the interview very rewarding.

Fortunately, however, I passed the interview and will be a vice judge for the winter break competition, learning how to decide cases systematically. The value of this was that I got to see the same event in a different perspective. When I was a competitor, I tended to focus too much on the case itself and the rules to the detriment of many issues. For example, the human and moral contradictions are reflected in the law. These issues should be carefully considered by all the law learners.
Moreover, I recently studied a book called A pale view of hill. It is a book about the rebuilding of Japan after World War II and describes how people who experienced the trauma of the war healed their inner selves. In literature class we watched a real video about the Tokyo trial and the international trial left me both in awe and excited. This was the real big case with just the statement being 10,000 pages long. As this history was far from me, I had no feelings of hatred and was able to hold a more objective attitude towards the defendants’ defence. I believe that my attitude is not indifferent to the issue of war. I hope to learn purely legal knowledge from it.
