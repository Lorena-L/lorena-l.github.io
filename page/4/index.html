The feeling about watching The silk

Posted on 2021-01-25

This week I have seen a new TV drama called the silk, recording the real life of how a normal lawyer get on well with her daily affairs. I always dream about be a counsel in the future, therefore I thought this TV series was helpful at first, but it’s more interesting than I thought in the beginning.

In this series, there are an amount of dimensions of lawyers’ life shown to the audience. The most intriguing part should be on court, because I have passion on researching the battles between witnesses and the lawyer. Counsel has to lay out the questions, like setting a trap, waiting for the witness to tell all the details, and find out where the problem was.

For instance, when Martha, the main character(a counsel). asking about if the witness is being raped or not, she did not ask her directly at first. Instead, she questioned where the witness were(at home), when she was doing at that time(showering), and asking about the relationship between her and her ex-boy friend. Actually, the ex and the witness had an appointment at witness’s house, so the problem is, why the witness knew her ex will come, but doing shower at that time? Why she didn’t dress up but only with a towel on when she opened the door? That is where the question came out.

Moreover, the series also shed some lights on the daily life of lawyers, their trouble and work in the office, the relationship between the colleges and how to deal with them. Sometimes you only have 1 day to prepare a case; and sometimes you have to replace your college when they are ill.

Another point is, it seems that the questions that were asked on the court were more simple than in the mock trial. I thought it is a good method to get the point, to make the points clear and straight forwards, without looking at the notes, that is really cool. I will try this method in this year’s tournament.

We introduce how to proceed a Mock Trial.

Procedure: Pre-trial matters by both sides

Opening statement by both sides

Direction examination by plaintiff (first witness)

Cross Examination by defense counsel

DX by plaintiff (2nd witness)

CX by defense

DX by plaintiff(3rd witness)

CX by defense

DX by defense，CX by plaintiff（etc）

Closing arguments by both sides

Rebuttle by the plaintiff

Court over.

The school has been started for a week and I finally attended the course which I selected last semester. Despite the class time and class work I had through the week, on the sunny afternoon on a chair, with bottom of milk tea I would love to have, art has never been such critical combination of my spare time.

My family always have a bias that doing art needs not much intelligence, and art can be only considered as an entertainment instead of a real work. To be honest, visual art really takes me a lot of time. And finally, I can have a whole afternoon enjoy impressionism in silent.

Breaking free of the naturalism of Impressionism in the late 1880s, a group of young painters sought independent artistic styles for expressing emotions rather than simply optical impressions, concentrating on themes of deeper symbolism.

Georges Seurat was at the forefront of the challenges to Impressionism with his unique analyses based on then-current notions of optical and color theories.

In 19th century, the impressionism was attacked by the classical artists, which be considered as a ridiculous style of painting, and impressionisms failed to exhibit their paintings through the official exhibitions. As the pioneer artists of impressionism with specific opinions in the match of colour based on main colour stream, like red, yellow, orange, originally, without any ingredients, to mix and create a vivid dream.

The representative, Claude Monet’s Impression, Sunrise (Musée Marmottan Monet, Paris) exhibited in 1874, gave the Impressionist movement its name when the critic Louis Leroy accused it of being a sketch or “impression,” not a finished painting. Although this style of art received a lot of crisis, it actually impressed the later genres and the whole Europe.

In addition, I thought literature can share some links with the impressionism as well. Using colours and brushes, artists have the capacity to build a world of imagination, and we prescribe the vivld image can also be created based on words. To be more specific, One Hundred Years of Solitude, impressed me with a complete generation of Latin America, based on such way of describing domestic affairs through one’s mental status, flow of mind, and ever-lasting changes of colours in the surroundings constructed by solitude human beings.
